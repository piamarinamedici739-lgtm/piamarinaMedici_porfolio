Abstract & Overview

MandaCare is a web-based complaint analysis system developed for the Mandaluyong City Health Department (CHD) to automate the processing, categorization, and prioritization of citizen complaints. In an era where digital technology is pivotal for public service efficiency, MandaCare addresses the limitations of outdated feedback mechanisms by integrating Natural Language Processing (NLP), Machine Learning, and Prescriptive Analytics.

The system was designed to assist the Public Information Office (PIO) in consolidating complaints. It transforms unstructured complaint text into actionable insights, detects urgency through Sentiment Analysis, and generates solution-oriented recommendations to improve the overall quality of public health services.

Key Objectives

  • Automated Categorization: To apply sentiment analysis and clustering techniques to categorize and prioritize complaints effectively.
  • Prescriptive Analytics: To integrate analytics that generate solution-oriented recommendations (prescriptions) based on the category and priority of the complaint.
  • Reporting & Dashboards: To provide a comprehensive dashboard for administrators to view trends, monitor statistics, and export detailed reports for official documentation.

Methodology & Algorithms

The development followed a Modified Waterfall SDLC. The core intelligence of the system relies on advanced data processing:

  • Unsupervised Clustering: Used to identify fifteen (15) validated complaint categories from raw data.
  • Supervised Learning: Four models were evaluated (Logistic Regression, Random Forest, SVM, and Ensemble). The Ensemble model was selected for deployment due to its balanced and reliable performance.
  • Sentiment Analysis: A fine-tuned multilingual model is used to determine the urgency of complaints, supporting automated priority assignment.
  • Prescriptive Engine: Automates the suggestion of courses of action based on a predefined prescription table derived from the complaint category and priority level.

Evaluation Results

The system was evaluated using the ISO 25010:2023 quality standard. It yielded strong results for both citizen and administrative access, with high ratings in Functional Suitability, Reliability, Security, Interaction Capability, and Flexibility. The evaluation confirmed that MandaCare is an effective, scalable, and secure solution for improving transparency and responsiveness in public health complaint management.
